Kitchens

We design our kitchens around three main styles each with their own unique feel and appearance. Click through to see some of our past work specially created to meet each customer’s needs, and budget.

Traditional

Warm, elegant and cozy, traditionally styled kitchens are timeless and inviting. Traditional kitchens emphasize symmetry, incorporating elaborate details such as:

  • Crown Molding, fluting, corbels and other ornamentation.
  • Raised panel door styles
  • Cherry, Maple, Walnut cabinetry
  • Vintage & Glazed finishes
  • Antique fixtures and appliances
  • Wood, stone or other natural materials

Transitional

Offering the best of both worlds, transitional kitchens include elements of both traditional and contemporary design equating to a classic look. Eclectic in nature, they mix natural and man-made materials as well as finishes and textures. Subtle accents draw the eye, adding flair without going overboard.

Contemporary

Also described as modern, clean and crisp contemporary kitchens are sleek and minimalistic, but far from plain and simple.

You can expect to see the following characteristics in a contemporary kitchen:

  • Horizontal lines
  • Textured melamine or wood grain veneers
  • White or bold color, Satin or high gloss finishes
  • Slab door style, lift up hardware
  • No moldings or ornamentation
  • Stainless steel and other metallic accents
  • Quartz countertops

Granite

Granite is a coarse grained crystalline igneous rock composed primarily of quartz and feldspar. It forms from slowly cooling magma that is subjected to extreme pressures deep beneath the earth's surface. The natural beauty and sophistication of granite makes it an easy choice for your kitchen remodel.

  • Naturally beautiful
  • Hard natural stone
  • Extremely durable
  • Scratch & Heat resistant
  • Wear resistant

Quartzite

Quartzite is a natural stone formed by the crystallization of sandstone due to heat and pressure underground. Some quartzite are opaque and some are so translucent that light can shine through them. In its’ purest form it’s white like marble, but can have impurities in it that lead to some incredible patterns and colors.

  • Naturally beautiful and stylish
  • Time durable
  • Natural hard rock
  • Uniform appearance

Marble

Marble is a hard crystalline metamorphic form of limestone, typically white with coloured mottling or streaks, which may be polished and is used in different objects and architecture.

  • Classic and elegant look
  • Distinct veining pattern
  • Durable when properly maintained
  • Great for bathrooms, backsplashes, fireplaces, furniture tops.

Quartz

Quartz is a man-made material that consists of 93% crushed quartz mixed with pigments and epoxy resin. Unlike natural stone, it doesn’t need periodic sealing, polishing or reconditioning. Because it's non-porous and non-absorbent, it won't draw in moisture from raw or leftover food, which can harbor harmful bacteria. A great choice where overhang is needed without support.

  • Naturally beautiful
  • Durability in time
  • Scratch resistant
  • Stain resistant
  • High heat resistant
  • No sealing or polishing needed
  • Easy to clean
